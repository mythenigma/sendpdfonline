What is a PDF QR Code?

A PDF QR code is a scannable code that opens a PDF link. Scan it with a phone camera and your document appears instantly — no typing, no extra apps.

It’s a simple bridge between printed materials and your digital files: scan and read.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is generating QR codes free?
A: Yes. MaiPDF lets you create QR codes for PDFs at no cost.
Q: Do QR codes expire?
A: They don’t expire by default, but you can set custom expiration dates for extra security.
Q: Can I track who scans my QR code?
A: Yes. You can see scan counts, approximate locations, and timestamps while respecting privacy.
Q: What's the file size limit?
A: MaiPDF supports PDF files up to 100MB, which covers most sharing needs.
Q: Can people download the PDF after scanning?
A: You control access — view-only or download-enabled, plus optional passwords and email verification.
Q: What if someone shares my QR code without permission?
A: You can disable or regenerate the QR code any time and set view limits to reduce misuse.

Best Practices

Do These:
  • Test the QR code before you share it
  • Make it large enough to scan (at least 2x2 cm when printed)
  • Add a short label that explains what the QR code links to
  • Use high contrast (dark code on a light background)
  • Set expiration dates for time-sensitive materials
Avoid These:
  • Printing QR codes too small to scan reliably
  • Using low-contrast colors that confuse scanners
  • Placing codes where they can be scratched or blocked
  • Forgetting to refresh expired QR codes
  • Sharing without testing first
