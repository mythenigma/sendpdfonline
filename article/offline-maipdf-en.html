Remotely Control Files Without Cloud: Lightweight Sharing of Locally Encrypted PDFs

Published: January 15, 2025 | Updated: December 03, 2025

Key selling point in one sentence: Package PDFs into local HTML, store on USB drives or NAS – each opening requires lightning-fast authorization from the server, ensuring confidentiality and revocability.

1 | Why Choose "Offline Storage + Online Authorization" Mode?

Traditional Approach Pain Points Offline Version Solution
Full Upload to Cloud Drives Data remains with third parties, high compliance pressure Files remain only in your hands or internal network
Pure Local Encryption Out of control once sent, cannot be revoked Each opening requires verification, can be locked at any time
Professional DRM Platform Complex deployment, high costs One-click HTML generation, affordable for individuals

2 | 4 Core Features of the Offline Version

Local Storage

PDFs are encapsulated into a single HTML, easily copied to USB drives / private NAS.

Online Verification

Background-level handshake upon opening: verifies reading code, remaining uses, expiration date. Disconnection → direct refusal to launch.

Read Code / Encode Dual Channel

Read Code: View only; Encode: Replace files, add uses. Retain a "regret button" while sharing files.

Access Tracking

Each verification request records IP, time, clear at a glance in the background – who has viewed, when it ended, one-click export to CSV.

3 | Typical Use Cases

Industry / Occasion Specific Use
Hardware Manufacturing Delivery Package blueprints into HTML to accompany equipment on-site; customer's computer has no internet? Bring your own portable Wi-Fi router to emit a one-time hotspot for verification.
Legal and Auditing Lawyers submit due diligence materials, limit to 5 uses with reading code; after the audit, "terminate authorization" with one click in the background using encoding.
Exhibition / Training Distribute product white papers via USB drive, automatically cache pages after initial verification, can still browse offline for 24 hours after leaving the exhibition hall.
University Research Internally exchange draft papers, files remain on the school's server; external collaborators can scan the QR code for temporary verification to view.

5 | Add Another Layer of Security Awareness

Non-Printable / Disable Copying: Browser-level disabling of print menus, right-click, and shortcuts.

Dynamic Watermark: Automatically overlays date + IP when opened, screenshots can also trace the source.

HTTPS Verification Channel: Authorization handshake uses an encrypted channel to prevent man-in-the-middle tampering.

One-Time Reading Mode: Deduct uses upon opening, invalidates when the window is closed, preventing screenshots and background streaming.
