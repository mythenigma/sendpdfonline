Watermarks have been used for centuries to protect documents — from currency bills to government papers. In the digital age, PDF watermarks serve the same purpose: they identify ownership, deter unauthorized copying, and trace document leaks. This guide covers everything from basic text watermarks to advanced dynamic watermarks that change based on who's viewing the document.

Watermark Types

Choose the Right Watermark for Your Needs

Dynamic Watermark

Shows viewer-specific information (IP address, timestamp, session ID) on every page. Each viewer sees a unique watermark, enabling leak tracing.

Static Text Watermark

Displays fixed text like "CONFIDENTIAL", "DRAFT", or your company name across all pages. Same for every viewer — ideal for branding and status marking.

Custom Text Watermark

Set any text you want — company name, project code, or recipient identifier. Applied semi-transparently across all pages at customizable opacity.

No-Download Protection

Combine watermarks with download blocking. Viewers can only see the watermarked document online — they can't save a clean copy.

Screenshot Deterrent

Dynamic watermarks with viewer info make screenshots traceable. Even if someone captures the screen, the watermark identifies who did it.

Leak Tracing

If a watermarked screenshot surfaces online, the embedded viewer info (IP, timestamp) traces the leak back to the exact person and session.
