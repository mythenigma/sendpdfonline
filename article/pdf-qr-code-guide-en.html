What is a PDF QR Code?

A PDF QR code is a quick response code that contains a link to your PDF document. When someone scans this QR code with their smartphone, they can instantly view your PDF file without needing to type any URLs or download apps.

Think of it as a bridge between your physical world and digital documents - just scan and view!

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is generating QR codes free?
A: Yes! MaiPDF provides completely free QR code generation for PDF files.
Q: Do QR codes expire?
A: By default, QR codes don't expire. However, you can set expiration dates (e.g., 7 days, 30 days) for added security.
Q: Can I track who scans my QR code?
A: Yes, you can see scan statistics including number of scans, approximate location, and time stamps while respecting user privacy.
Q: What's the file size limit?
A: MaiPDF supports PDF files up to 100MB, which covers most document sharing needs.
Q: Can people download the PDF after scanning?
A: You have full control - allow viewing only, or enable downloads. You can also require passwords or email verification.
Q: What if someone shares my QR code without permission?
A: You can disable or regenerate QR codes at any time, and set view limits to prevent overuse.

Best Practices

Do These:
  • Test your QR code before sharing to ensure it works
  • Make QR codes large enough to scan easily (at least 2x2 cm when printed)
  • Add a brief description near the QR code explaining what it contains
  • Use high contrast colors (dark QR code on light background)
  • Set appropriate expiration dates for time-sensitive content
Avoid These:
  • Making QR codes too small to scan reliably
  • Using low contrast color combinations
  • Placing QR codes where they might get damaged or obscured
  • Forgetting to update expired QR codes
  • Sharing QR codes without testing them first
