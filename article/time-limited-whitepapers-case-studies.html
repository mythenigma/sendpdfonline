Share Valuable Content—Without Giving It Away Forever

Explore PDF Time Limits

Protecting Your Marketing Assets

Whitepapers. Case studies. Strategy PDFs. These are some of your most powerful marketing tools—but also some of the hardest to control once they're shared.

You send a link, attach a file, or run a gated download campaign. People get the content. But then:

  • It's downloaded and stored indefinitely
  • Forwarded outside your target audience
  • Uploaded to forums or shared by competitors
  • Referenced long after the content is out of date

Your valuable insights live on—but often out of your control.

Why Time-Limited Access Matters

For marketers and brand teams, control = credibility.

You want to:

  • Limit how long a whitepaper stays live
  • Prevent old documents from being reused
  • Test interest without permanent exposure
  • Create urgency (e.g. "Available for 72 hours only")
  • Avoid becoming a source for your competitors

"A simple download link doesn't give you that power. But controlled, time-limited viewing does."

The Better Way: Controlled PDF Viewing with Expiry Logic

Instead of attaching your document or giving unrestricted access, use a platform that lets you:

1

Generate view-only links or QR codes

Create restricted access points that don't allow downloads

2

Set precise limits

E.g., open only 3 times, or view once then expire

3

Disable download and print

Prevent unauthorized copying and distribution

4

Monitor engagement

See who opened it, when, and where

5

Revoke or update access

Change permissions later—without resending anything

Pro Tip

It's smarter. Cleaner. And way more professional than basic PDF sharing.

Real-World Example

You're launching a limited campaign for your new AI platform. Part of the campaign: a detailed whitepaper explaining your edge.

You don't want:

  • The content being downloaded and shared indefinitely
  • Old versions circulating months later
  • Competitors using your insights against you

With a smart sharing tool:

  1. You upload the PDF
  2. Set the link to allow 2 opens per user
  3. Disable downloads
  1. Share via email or a campaign QR code
  2. Remove access a week later, automatically or manually

You control the window of visibility, not just the distribution.
