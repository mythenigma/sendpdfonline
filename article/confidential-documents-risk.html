The Critical Risks of Traditional Document Sharing

Sharing confidential documents through traditional methods (email, cloud storage, file sharing services) exposes your organization to significant security risks. Understanding these risks is the first step toward implementing proper security measures.

Unauthorized Access

Once shared via email or unsecured links, you lose control over who can access your documents. Links can be forwarded, shared on social media, or accessed by unauthorized parties. A single compromised link can expose sensitive information to countless individuals.

Uncontrolled Sharing

Documents can be forwarded, copied, or shared without your knowledge or consent. Recipients may share confidential information with competitors, unauthorized third parties, or post it publicly, causing irreparable damage to your business.

Unrestricted Downloads

Anyone with access can download and store your confidential documents permanently. Downloaded files can be distributed offline, making it impossible to revoke access or track usage. This creates permanent security vulnerabilities.

Unauthorized Copying

Traditional PDF sharing allows unlimited copying, printing, and screenshot capture. Confidential information can be extracted and used without your knowledge, leading to intellectual property theft and competitive disadvantage.

Permanent Access

Once shared, documents remain accessible indefinitely. There's no way to revoke access after sharing, even if circumstances change or the recipient's authorization is revoked. This creates ongoing security risks.

No Visibility

Traditional sharing methods provide no visibility into who accessed documents, when they were viewed, or how they were used. This lack of tracking makes it impossible to detect unauthorized access or investigate security incidents.

Best Practices for Confidential Document Security

Security Configuration Best Practices

  • Always Use Password Protection: Require passwords for all confidential documents, even when shared with trusted recipients
  • Set Strict View Limits: For highly confidential documents, limit views to 1-2 per recipient to prevent excessive access
  • Use Short Expiration Dates: Set expiration dates close to when access is needed (e.g., 7-30 days) rather than leaving documents accessible indefinitely
  • Enable All Protection Features: Use password protection, view limits, expiration dates, watermarks, and DRM together for maximum security
  • Share Passwords Separately: Never include passwords in the same message as the document link. Share passwords through a different secure channel

Recipient Management

  • Verify Recipient Identity: Confirm recipient identity before sharing confidential documents
  • Limit Recipient Count: Only share with necessary recipients. Each additional recipient increases security risk
  • Document Recipient List: Keep a record of who has access to confidential documents
  • Regular Access Reviews: Periodically review who has access and revoke access for recipients who no longer need it

Monitoring and Alerts

  • Enable Access Alerts: Set up notifications for document access to detect unauthorized viewing
  • Review Analytics Regularly: Check analytics dashboard frequently to identify suspicious access patterns
  • Investigate Anomalies: Investigate any unusual access patterns, such as access from unexpected locations or times
  • Document Security Incidents: Maintain records of security incidents and responses for compliance and improvement
