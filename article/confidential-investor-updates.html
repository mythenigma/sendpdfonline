Why Secure Investor Documents?

Investor updates and pitch decks are confidential. A single forwarded link can leak to competitors or the wrong party. With verification, view limits, watermarks, and expiry you keep control and can see who actually opened the file—so you follow up with the right people.

Controls

Recommended Investor Security Stack

Email verification

Allow only known recipients. You see who opened in access records.

View limits

Cap repeated opens so the link isn’t shared endlessly.

Dynamic watermark

Show viewer identity on the PDF to deter screenshots and leaks.

Expiry

Auto-close access after the update window (e.g. board meeting).

Example

What Investors Actually See

Before opening the PDF, recipients may be asked to enter their email. After opening, you can review access records to see who viewed and when.

Tips

Best practices

  • Always enable email verification for investor-only updates so opens are tied to identity.
  • Set view limits (e.g. 3–5 opens) and expiry (e.g. 7 days) so links don’t live forever.
  • Use the watermark on high-sensitivity materials to discourage screenshots.
  • Check access records before the next meeting to see who engaged with the deck.
